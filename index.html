About

About Vi

I'm an engineer, hardware developer, and composer in Little Italy, San Diego. I build things end to end: the code, the circuit board, the CAD, the music, and the curriculum to teach all of it.

At Treobytes I designed and shipped a production Claude AI agent system, solo, that turns live data into compliant, submission-ready documents with RAG and an automated LLM-as-judge review, cutting turnaround from four days to five hours. I built the team's internal React platform from the ground up, wrote analysis pipelines that turn unstructured documents into scored prospects, and designed an Arduino MIDI instrument as a buildable STEM kit.

I've showcased an adaptive game-audio tool at GDC San Francisco, scored 17 games and 14 film and TV projects, earning Silver for Best Composer at the 2022 Tokyo Film Awards, and seen my computational design research featured in Dezeen. I hold a B.S. in Applied Computer Science (Media Arts) from Woodbury University, Magna Cum Laude with a 3.8 GPA and the Best Thesis Award, and I teach GAME 203: Sound Design Fundamentals as an adjunct professor.

I'm happiest at the seam between disciplines: applied AI inside a real product, or audio code talking to a circuit board. I'm currently building CADence, a browser 3D modeler.
