Hardware & CAD2026 · in progress

CADence

A 3D modeling app in the browser that makes precise, professional-grade CAD as approachable as a toy.

Solid modeling with exact dimensions and clean file export, plus the thing beginner and pro tools both miss: switching between freeform sculpting and precise, editable design on the fly.

JavaScriptThree.jsWebGLGeometry kernels
Live demo
Problem · Approach

ProblemBeginner 3D tools are easy but imprecise; professional CAD is precise but intimidating, and neither lets you move freely between freeform sculpting and exact, editable design.ApproachEvery shape is stored as a recipe of exact parameters, and the 3D model is generated from that recipe. Keeping that history is what makes switching between freeform and precise modeling possible.

About

About Vi

I'm a full-stack engineer, hardware product developer, and composer based in Little Italy, San Diego. What ties my work together is simple: I build the whole thing. Code, circuit, CAD, sound, and the curriculum to teach it.

At Treobytes, I designed and shipped a production three-layer Claude AI agent system, solo, in five days, that non-engineers now use to do real expert work. I also built the team's internal React platform from the ground up, wrote automation pipelines that turn unstructured public documents into scored prospects, and took an Arduino MIDI instrument from breadboard to a 100+ unit manufacturing run.

That range isn't an accident. I've showcased a generative game-audio tool at GDC San Francisco, scored 17 games and 14 films and TV projects, earning Silver for Best Composer at the 2022 Tokyo Film Awards, and seen my computational-design research featured in Dezeen. I hold a B.S. in Applied Computer Science, Media Arts from the University of Redlands (Magna Cum Laude, Best Thesis Award), and I teach Sound Design Fundamentals at Woodbury University.

I'm happiest at the seam between disciplines, where applied AI meets real products, where software meets hardware, where precision meets craft. I'm currently building CADence, a browser 3D modeler.
