Hardware & CAD2026 · in progress

CADence

A browser 3D modeler fusing TinkerCAD's ease with OnShape's precision — and a mesh↔parametric swap neither tool allows.

Working primitives, boolean solids/holes, a precise numeric inspector, STL/3MF export, and a validated headless B-rep export pipeline.

JavaScriptThree.jsManifoldOpenCascadeWebGL
Details

Problem. Beginner tools are easy but imprecise; pro CAD is precise but steep — and neither moves fluidly between sculptural and parametric modeling.

Approach. Every object carries a recipe; the mesh is generated from it, never the source of truth — which makes the sculptural↔parametric swap tractable.

About

About Vi

I'm a full-stack engineer, hardware product developer, and composer based in Little Italy, San Diego. What ties my work together is simple: I build the whole thing. Code, circuit, CAD, sound — and the curriculum to teach it.

At Treobytes, I designed and shipped a production three-layer Claude AI agent system — solo, in five days — that non-engineers now use to do real expert work. I also built the team's internal React platform from the ground up, wrote automation pipelines that turn unstructured public documents into scored prospects, and took an Arduino MIDI instrument from breadboard to a 100+ unit manufacturing run.

That range isn't an accident. I've showcased a generative game-audio tool at GDC San Francisco, scored 17 games and 14 films and TV projects — earning Silver for Best Composer at the 2022 Tokyo Film Awards — and seen my computational-design research featured in Dezeen and the LA Times. I hold a B.S. in Applied Computer Science — Media Arts from the University of Redlands (Magna Cum Laude, Best Thesis Award), and I teach Sound Design Fundamentals at Woodbury University.

I'm happiest at the seam between disciplines — where applied AI meets real products, where software meets hardware, where precision meets craft. I'm currently building CADence, a browser 3D modeler.
