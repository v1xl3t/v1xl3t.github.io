Claude AI Skill Tree

A production AI agent system on the Claude API that generates compliant, submission-ready proposals and grant documents from live data via RAG. Designed and shipped solo.

Cut team turnaround from four days to five hours, with an automated LLM-as-judge review flagging policy and brand-guideline violations before anything reaches a client.

Claude APIRAGLLM-as-judgePythonJSON schema
Problem · Approach

ProblemA small team needed compliant, submission-ready documents far faster than people could produce them by hand, and without hiring engineers to operate the system.ApproachA three-layer agent architecture: reusable primitives compose into domain skills, which compose into full workflows. RAG pulls live data, and an automated LLM-as-judge review gates every output against policy and brand guidelines before delivery, so the system scales to non-technical users without becoming brittle.

JavaScriptThree.jsWebGLGeometry kernelB-rep
Live demo
Problem · Approach

ProblemBeginner 3D tools are easy but imprecise; professional CAD is precise but intimidating, and neither lets you move freely between freeform sculpting and exact, editable design.ApproachEvery shape is stored as a recipe of exact parameters, and the 3D model is generated from that recipe. Hardware & CADTreobytes

Arduino MIDI Keyboard (TEMPLiT)

A buildable MIDI keyboard kit that teaches students to wire real circuitry, designed end to end from CAD to the breadboard and safe for a classroom.

Students assemble it themselves with TEMPLiT overlays that guide real components onto a breadboard over an accurate schematic. Plug-and-play with no drivers, and shipped to classrooms.

Arduino LeonardoUSB-HID firmwareC++OnShape CADLaser cuttingSTEM kit design
Problem · Approach

ProblemAn affordable music controller a student could build themselves, plug-and-play, and safe and simple enough for a classroom.ApproachDesigned the circuit and wrote the USB-HID firmware so it is recognized as a plug-and-play MIDI device with no drivers. Designed the enclosure in CAD for laser-cut assembly, and designed the kit's TEMPLiT overlays: semi-transparent boards that sit over a breadboard with holes at each schematic symbol, so students place real components directly onto an accurate schematic as they build.

About

About Vi

I'm a full-stack engineer, hardware product developer, and composer based in Little Italy, San Diego. What ties my work together is simple: I build the whole thing. Code, circuit, CAD, sound, and the curriculum to teach it.

At Treobytes I designed and shipped a production Claude AI agent system, solo, that turns live data into compliant, submission-ready documents with RAG and an automated LLM-as-judge review, cutting turnaround from four days to five hours. I built the team's internal React platform from the ground up, wrote analysis pipelines that turn unstructured documents into scored prospects, and took an Arduino MIDI instrument from breadboard to a 100+ unit manufacturing run.

That range isn't an accident. I've showcased a generative game-audio tool at GDC San Francisco, scored 17 games and 14 film and TV projects, earning Silver for Best Composer at the 2022 Tokyo Film Awards, and seen my computational-design research featured in Dezeen. I hold a B.S. in Applied Computer Science (Media Arts) from Woodbury University, Magna Cum Laude with a 3.8 GPA and the Best Thesis Award, and I teach GAME 203: Sound Design Fundamentals as an adjunct professor.

I'm happiest at the seam between disciplines, where applied AI meets real products, where software meets hardware, where precision meets craft. I'm currently building CADence, a browser 3D modeler.
